The Classic of Mountains and Seas

Introduction

The Classic of Mountains and Seas, although with only more than 31000 words in all, is indeed all-encompassing. In addition to myths and legends, it also involves geography, history, religion, folklore, calendars, astronomy, animals, plants, minerals, medicine, anthropology, ethnology, geology and oceanography. This is a wonderful book, an encyclopedia of the daily life of the ancient people. The Classic of Mountains and Seas can indeed be called an "ancient wizard book" which preserves so many valuable materials of myths and legends. Whatâ€™s more valuable is that these materials of myths and legends are close to the original state, without much modification. To study The Classic of Mountains and Seas is not only to discover grotesque stories and characters, but also to learn the imagination and creativity of the ancients, as all those fantasies that look ridiculous have a profound meaning behind them. For example, they are surprised at but unable to explain the various phenomena that occur in nature, such as the wind, rain, thunder and lightning, the burning of fires in the forest, the movement of the sun and the moon, so they think they are all living things and call them gods. Moreover, the quality of primitive labor force is low, so they create songs about labor and labor heroes, eulogizing Pangu who created the earth with an axe, Nuwa who created human beings and refined five-colored stones to repair the sky, Shennong who discovered herbs, Houji who taught people how to plant their crops, and Dayu who governed the flood. These labor heroes, who conquered nature and improved human life, are most worshipped by people. From this we can see that those myths that seem bizarre and illogical are based on real life, not from fantasy in the human mind. From the above facts, we can clearly know that, in essence, deepening, like other arts, reflects a certain social life. It is a superstructure produced on a certain social basis, and is an art of ideological form.

Four chapters of The Classic of Great Wilderness and one chapter of The Classic within the Four Seas were the first to be written in about the early to middle Warring States period (the fourth century BC)

Five chapters of The Classic of Mountains and four chapters of The Classic Overseas were written later after the middle Warring States period (the third century BC)

Four chapters of The Classic within the Four Seas were the latest to be written in about the early Han Dynasty (the former Han with the date of 6 BC)
